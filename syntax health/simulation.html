How simulation works

You send current health data (vitals, labs, symptoms, medications). Our engine updates a virtual health state and projects how that state may change over time. Results are returned in under 2 seconds for use in dashboards and alerts.

Inputs

Vitals (BP, heart rate), mood and sleep scores, iron intake, activity level, pain, feeding and weight (for newborns). Data can be real-time or historical.

Models

Per-system models (cardio, nervous, digestive, muscular, optional skeletal) run in parallel. Maternal and newborn models are linked where relevant.

Outputs

Risk scores (low / moderate / high), trajectory curves, alert flags, and a unified digital twin state for visualization and the API.

Body system modules

Each module simulates one aspect of physiology. Together they form a single digital twin; each can be used or updated independently.

Cardiovascular: BP, heart rate, swelling, headache. Maternal cardiovascular risk and future trajectory; alerts when thresholds are exceeded.
Nervous system: mood score, sleep hours, stress. Emotional trend, postpartum depression risk (PHQ-style), 30-day mental health projection.
Digestive and nutritional: iron intake, appetite, hydration. Anemia risk, fatigue progression, and nutritional intervention signals.
Muscular recovery: activity level and pain. Postpartum recovery strength and delayed-recovery risk.
Skeletal (optional): calcium intake and bone strength trend for at-risk populations.
Maternal and newborn: pregnancy/postpartum stage, delivery type, infant birth weight; feeding, temperature, weight. Maternal risk index and newborn vulnerability score.

Scenario types

Simulations support two main scenario types so clinicians and patients can compare outcomes.

If trend continues: projects risk and trajectory assuming current behavior and no new intervention. Surfaces where and when risk may rise.
If intervention occurs: projects risk and trajectory after a chosen intervention (e.g. medication change, nutrition, rest, clinic visit). Enables side-by-side comparison with the baseline scenario.
Comparative view: both scenarios can be shown together in the dashboard and API so users see the impact of acting now vs. not acting.

Performance and scale

Simulation is built for speed and scalability without sacrificing accuracy.

Response time: simulation results in under 2 seconds so dashboards and alerts feel real-time.
Modular design: each body system is simulated in its own module so we can tune, update, or extend one system without rewriting the rest.
Scale: from a single clinic to national-level maternal or chronic-disease monitoring; architecture supports high concurrency and batch runs.
